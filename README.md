# fusion-economics-research
Research tool and economic model to determine viability of different fusion reactor designs.

## Title

Evaluating the Economic Viability of Major Fusion Reactor Designs Through Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis

## Research Question

Which currently proposed fusion reactor design has the greatest potential to become economically viable for commercial electricity generation by 2050?

## Introduction

Nuclear fusion has long been considered a promising source of clean and abundant energy. Recent breakthroughs in plasma confinement, superconducting magnets, and private-sector investment have accelerated the development of multiple competing fusion technologies. However, significant uncertainty remains regarding which reactor design is most likely to achieve commercial success.

Current research primarily focuses on the scientific feasibility of fusion reactors. Comparatively little attention has been devoted to systematically evaluating the economic viability of competing reactor designs. This study seeks to address that gap by comparing major fusion approaches using a quantitative decision-analysis framework.

## Purpose and Significance

The purpose of this study is to determine which fusion reactor design demonstrates the strongest economic potential based on technological readiness, scalability, projected costs, and operational considerations.

The findings may contribute to discussions regarding future energy investment and provide a framework for comparing emerging energy technologies.

## Fusion Technologies Examined

### Tokamak
### Stellarator
### Inertial Confinement Fusion
### Magnetized Target Fusion
### Pulsed Magneto-Inertial Fusion

## Hypothesis

Fusion reactor designs with lower engineering complexity and higher technology readiness levels will demonstrate greater economic viability than designs that require significant scientific or engineering breakthroughs before commercialization.

## Methodology Overview

This study will employ a Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) framework.

Each reactor design will be evaluated using several criteria:

Capital Cost
Operating Cost
Technology Readiness
Scalability
Fuel Availability
Maintenance Requirements

Each criterion will be assigned a weighted value based on its importance to commercial deployment.

A composite Fusion Viability Index (FVI) will be calculated for each reactor design.

## Data Sources

Data will be collected from:

Peer-reviewed journal articles
National laboratories
Government energy agencies
Fusion companies
Expert interviews

## Sensitivity Analysis

Alternative weighting scenarios will be tested to determine how changes in economic priorities affect reactor rankings.

### Examples include:

Cost-focused scenario
Scalability-focused scenario
Technology-readiness-focused scenario

## Expected Outcomes

The study is expected to identify which reactor design currently appears most capable of achieving economic competitiveness and to determine which variables most strongly influence commercial success.
